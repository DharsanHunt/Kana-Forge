Lesson 01: The Particle は (Wa)

The absolute foundation of Japanese sentence structure. Mastering the topic marker は allows you to define exactly what your sentence is about.

Understanding the Topic Marker

In Japanese, particles are functional words that follow a noun to indicate its relationship to the verb. The particle は marks the "Topic" of the sentence. It can be translated as "As for..." or "Regarding...".

  • Identifies the subject or theme being discussed.
  • Pronounced as 'wa' despite being written with the hiragana 'ha'.
  • Essential for basic "A is B" sentence patterns.

Practice Examples

私は学生です。 Watashi wa gakusei desu. I am a student. (As for me, student am.)
これはペンです。 Kore wa pen desu. This is a pen.
今日は暑いです。 Kyō wa atsui desu. Today is hot.
info Cultural Note

In Japanese conversation, once the topic (marked by は) is established, it is often omitted in subsequent sentences if it remains the same. This makes Japanese highly context-dependent compared to English.
